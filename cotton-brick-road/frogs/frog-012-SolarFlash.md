## Educational Content

### Vocabulary: Amphibian

> **Amphibian** comes from Greek "amphi" (both) + "bios" (life) = "double life." Amphibians live part of their life in water and part on land.

### Characteristics of Amphibians

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Skin** | Moist, smooth, no scales |
| **Breathing** | Gills as babies, lungs as adults (most) |
| **Eggs** | Soft, jelly-like, laid in water |
| **Body temp** | Cold-blooded (needs external heat) |
| **Metamorphosis** | Dramatic body change (tadpole → frog) |

### Examples
- Frogs and toads
- Salamanders and newts
- Caecilians (legless amphibians)

## Biology Fact

Solar Flash's bright yellow color is **aposematic** — it's a warning signal. But did you know? Dart frogs aren't born poisonous! They get their toxins from **eating poisonous insects** in the wild. In captivity, with a different diet, they lose their toxicity!

## Solar Connection

Why "Solar Flash"?
- Bright yellow = sunlight
- Speckles = solar flares
- Basking behavior = needs warmth (cold-blooded)
- Active in daytime = diurnal

This contrasts with nocturnal amphibians that come out at night!
