## Educational Fact

> **Camouflage vs. Warning Colors:** While some dart frogs advertise toxicity with bright colors, others use **camouflage** to hide from predators. Green frogs like this one blend into moss and leaves, ambushing tiny insects. It's a different survival strategy than the "don't eat me!" approach.

---

## Biology Note
Many dart frog species show incredible diversity in color morphs — the same species can be bright yellow in one valley and deep green in the next, depending on local predator pressures and available food sources.
