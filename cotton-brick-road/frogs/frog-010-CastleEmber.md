## Educational Fact

> **Territorial Behavior:** Male dart frogs are fiercely territorial, defending prime egg-laying spots from rivals. The "Castle Ember" represents this territorial drive — guarding something precious (the castle gate) just as real frogs guard their breeding sites.

---

## Biology Note
Male dart frogs have elaborate courtship rituals including calling, chasing, and even wrestling other males. The most colorful males often have the best territories — their bright colors advertise both toxicity AND fitness to potential mates!
